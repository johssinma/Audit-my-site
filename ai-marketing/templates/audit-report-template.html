01 — Executive Summary

At a Glance

Nike.com scores 85/100 — a near-elite marketing presence built on one of the world's most recognised brand identities. The "Just Do It" tagline communicates instantly and the membership ecosystem creates strong retention. However, the homepage lacks a benefit-driven H1 above the fold for first-time visitors, and social proof (customer reviews, star ratings) surfaces too deep in the funnel. Addressing these two gaps could meaningfully lift conversion for new visitor acquisition.

04 — Recommendations

Priority Action Plan

01
Add a benefit-driven H1 above the fold
Replace the current visual-only hero with a headline that speaks to a specific desire. Example: "Built for athletes who refuse to stop." This gives first-time visitors a reason to stay.
HIGH IMPACT Low effort · 1 day
02
Surface social proof on the homepage hero
Add "4.8★ rated by 12,000+ athletes" as a trust badge directly below the primary CTA. This single change can lift click-through by 15-25% for new visitors.
HIGH IMPACT Low effort · 2 hours
03
Reduce mega-menu to top 6 categories
Progressive disclosure: show 6 top-level categories, let users drill down. Fewer choices at the top level reduces decision paralysis and bounce rate, especially on mobile.
MEDIUM IMPACT Medium effort · 1 week
04
Enrich meta descriptions on product pages
Replace auto-generated meta descriptions with hand-crafted versions that include the product's key benefit and a CTA. Improves organic click-through rate by an estimated 10-20%.
MEDIUM IMPACT Low effort · Ongoing
